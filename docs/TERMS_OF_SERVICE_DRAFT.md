## 2. What IIVO Glass Does

IIVO Glass is an AI-powered overlay application for macOS that:

- Captures system audio from your device (podcasts, videos, meetings, lectures)
- Transcribes audio using third-party speech-to-text services
- Generates real-time AI notes, summaries, and insights from transcripts
- Displays an interactive overlay on your screen during audio sessions
- Optionally captures screenshots of your screen for visual context features
- Supports voice commands processed by AI
- Translates audio content in real time
- (Future) Integrates with video conferencing tools (Zoom, Google Meet, Microsoft Teams)
- (Future) Syncs notes and summaries across devices and to third-party applications

The IIVO Lens browser extension captures browser tab audio and page context to enhance the above features.

IIVO Glass can capture audio from your device, including audio from phone calls, video conferences, and in-person conversations. **Recording laws vary significantly by jurisdiction.** By using the Service, you represent and warrant that:

- You have obtained all legally required consents from all parties being recorded

- You will not use the Service to record any conversation without the knowledge of all parties where required by law

### 3.2 U.S. Recording Law Overview (Not Legal Advice)

United States federal law (the Electronic Communications Privacy Act, 18 U.S.C. § 2511) permits recording of a conversation if **at least one party consents** (one-party consent). However, the following states and territories require **all parties to consent** before recording:

California, Connecticut, Delaware, Florida, Illinois, Maryland, Massachusetts, Michigan, Montana, Nevada, New Hampshire, Oregon, Pennsylvania, Washington, and others.

If you are in, or recording a party who is in, a two-party/all-party consent jurisdiction, you must obtain consent from all participants before recording.

### 3.3 International Users

Recording laws in other countries may be stricter. EU users should be aware that recording personal data via audio may trigger obligations under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). Users in Canada, Australia, the UK, and other jurisdictions should consult applicable local law.

### 3.4 Meetings and Conferences

When using IIVO Glass during a video conference (Zoom, Google Meet, Teams, etc.):

- You should disclose to all participants that AI note-taking is active

- **Audio** is captured from your device's system audio output and/or microphone
